🎬 Intro

Introduction

Sets the mood and establishes the sonic identity. Usually 4–8 bars. Should hint at the energy and style of the whole song without giving everything away.

📖 Verse

Verse

Tells the story. Typically 8–16 bars. Lower energy than the chorus. Builds narrative and tension that resolves in the chorus. Vocal melodies here tend to be conversational.

⬆️ Pre-Chorus

Pre-Chorus

The build. Usually 4–8 bars. Lifts energy from the verse toward the chorus. Creates anticipation. Often features rising melodies or a rhythmic shift.

🎆 Chorus

Chorus

The emotional peak. 8–16 bars. Highest energy, most memorable hook. Repeated throughout the song. Suno needs this to be defined clearly to maintain consistency.

🪝 Hook

Hook

A short, ultra-memorable phrase. 2–4 bars. Can be vocal or instrumental. Different from a chorus — it's a recurring motif rather than a full section.

🌉 Bridge

Bridge

Contrast section. 4–8 bars. Appears once, usually after the second chorus. Different chord progression, melody and feel from everything else. Provides emotional release before the final chorus.

💥 Breakdown

Breakdown

Strips back the production dramatically. 2–4 bars. Common in EDM, rock and hip-hop. Creates tension and space before a drop or chorus hit.

🎸 Solo

Solo

Instrumental spotlight. 4–8 bars. Features a lead instrument (guitar, synth, sax etc.) playing melodically over the backing track. Common in rock, jazz and funk.

🎹 Instrumental

Instrumental

Extended instrumental passage. 8–16 bars. No vocals. Can be a groove section, a production showcase, or a reprieve before vocals return.

🎬 Outro

Outro

The ending. 4–8 bars. Can fade out gradually, end on a resolved chord, or go cold. Sets how the listener feels as the song ends.

🏛️ Suno Sliders Guide

The Three Suno Sliders

Understanding these sliders is key to getting consistent, high-quality results. Keep them identical across all parts of a split generation.

🌀 Weirdness

Low (0–30): Stays within your prompt guidelines. Safe but can sound flat or generic.

Mid (30–60): Makes some creative stylistic decisions. Best balance. Recommended starting point.

High (60–100): Bold creative choices, evolving instrumentation. ⚠️ Above 70 may cause stylistic drift — instruments and style can wander from your prompt.


🎨 Style / Clarity

Low (0–30): Loose interpretation of style tags. May blend genres unexpectedly.

Mid (30–60): Balanced adherence to your style prompt. Good for most songs.

High (60–100): Strict adherence to tags. Can sound rigid at very high settings.


🎵 Intensity / Energy

Low (0–30): Restrained, subtle. Good for ballads and ambient.

Mid (30–60): Natural dynamic range. Recommended for most genres.

High (60–100): Dense, loud, high-energy. Great for EDM/metal — can over-compress softer tracks.


💡 SoundVektor Tip: For multi-part generations, keep all three sliders identical across parts. Even a small Weirdness change between Part 1 and Part 2 will cause audible drift.
